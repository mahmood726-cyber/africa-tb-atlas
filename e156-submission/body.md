We audited modern MDR-TB trials using bedaquiline, pretomanid, or linezolid since regulatory approval for African representation in Cochrane synthesis.
Of 72 MDR/XDR-TB regimen trials registered in ClinicalTrials.gov since 2012-12-28 (bedaquiline FDA-EUA), 44 (61%) included at least one African site, recruiting 14,931 of 19,614 trial-arm participants.
Each trial passed a four-gate funnel (registration, results-posting, Europe PMC publication, Cochrane via Pairwise70 plus a v0.1.1 supplement of scraped TB-Cochrane reviews), pre-registered via signed GitHub tag and Internet Archive snapshot.
African-recruiting trials achieved peer-publication at 90.9% versus 71.4% non-Africa, inverting north-south discipline narratives.
Cochrane inclusion was 1/44 (2.3%) Africa-recruiting versus 0/28 non-Africa; African-led trials reached 3.4% (1/29) versus 0% for African-recruiting and non-Africa, a directional asymmetry.
The match is the African-led STAND trial (NCT02342886) in CD012918; modest counts reflect partial TB-Cochrane reference coverage.
