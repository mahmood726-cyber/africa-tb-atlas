We audited modern MDR-TB trials using bedaquiline, pretomanid, or linezolid since regulatory approval for African-site representation in Cochrane synthesis.
Of 72 MDR/XDR-TB regimen trials registered in ClinicalTrials.gov since 2012-12-28 (bedaquiline FDA-EUA), 44 (61%) included at least one African site, recruiting 14,931 of 19,614 trial-arm participants.
Each trial passed a four-gate funnel (registration, results-posting, Europe PMC publication, Cochrane via Pairwise70 NCT-bridge or CDSR string), pre-registered via signed GitHub tag and Internet Archive snapshot.
African-recruiting trials achieved peer-publication at 90.9% versus 71.4% for non-Africa, inverting north-south publication-discipline narratives, reflecting concentrated TB-Alliance/USAID/EDCTP funding.
Cochrane inclusion registered 0/72 across all strata; investigation traced this to a reference-data gap, not methodological pathology — Pairwise70 and CDSR string-index collectively carry zero modern-MDR-TB Cochrane reviews.
