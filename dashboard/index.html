Africa TB Atlas

Hidden evidence in Africa-recruiting tuberculosis trials — Gate 0 to Gate 3 funnel

This atlas quantifies the proportion of Africa-recruiting drug-resistant TB trials (registered on ClinicalTrials.gov / WHO ICTRP) whose evidence reaches Cochrane systematic reviews (Gate 3). Evidence hidden at intermediate gates — results posting (G1), peer publication (G2), Cochrane inclusion (G3) — represents a structural gap between trial conduct and evidence synthesis uptake.

Methods note

Data source. ClinicalTrials.gov / WHO ICTRP export (fixture-mode: tests/fixtures/atlas_baseline_micro.csv). Atlas generated by the africa-tb-atlas pipeline (preregistered at prereg-v0.0.1). The atlas CSV is committed byte-for-byte and anchored via OpenTimestamps.

Stratifications. Four sensitivity analyses are reported: (1) binary_1site — whether any trial site is in Africa (binary); (2) site_share_30pct — whether ≥30% of trial sites are African; (3) three_tier — African-led vs African-recruiting vs non-Africa; (4) drug_class — regimen class (BPaL, BPaLM, Bdq-based, linezolid, pretomanid-based, other).

Gates. G0 = registered trial; G1 = results posted on CT.gov; G2 = peer-reviewed publication detected via PubMed / Europe PMC NCT-bridge; G3 = included in a Cochrane systematic review (CDSR match). The primary outcome is pct_g0_to_g3 (% of registered trials reaching Cochrane).

CIs. 95% cluster bootstrap confidence intervals (1,000 resamples, trial-level clusters). Undefined CIs (k=1) shown as point-only.

Spec & protocol. See docs/spec.md and docs/protocol.md. Preregistration tag: prereg-v0.0.1.
